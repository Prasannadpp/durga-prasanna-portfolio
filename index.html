About Me

Building Scalable Backends at the Intersection of Data and AI

I am a Backend Software Engineer with 5+ years of experience building production-grade distributed backend systems for data-intensive and AI-enabled applications. My focus is on designing systems that scale under concurrent load, remain observable in production, and evolve safely over time.

My experience spans two complementary environments. At Infosys, I led distributed Python microservices handling 100K+ daily transactions, with ownership of service reliability, fault isolation, and zero-downtime data migrations for 200K+ records. At Texas Tech, I operationalized research workflows by building RAG pipelines, asynchronous processing systems, and analytics backends that transformed complex data into actionable insights.

I specialize in clean system design, asynchronous processing, and medallion-style data architectures. I have completed my MS in Computer Science and am actively seeking backend engineering roles where distributed systems, data integrity, and scalability are top priorities.
